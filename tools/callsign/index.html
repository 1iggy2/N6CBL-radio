About this lookup
DXCC is the ARRL-maintained list of “entities” recognized for the DX Century Club award — roughly 340 distinct political and geographic units. Each entity has a numeric code, an assigned continent, and falls in a specific ITU and CQ zone. Prefix matching here is heuristic: this tool walks a hand-curated list of ~180 common prefixes (longest match wins) and will not catch every special-event call, IOTA island designator, or rare entity. Portable suffixes (/MM maritime mobile, /AM aeronautical mobile, /P portable, /M mobile) are stripped before lookup, so a /P doesn’t change the home country. CQ and ITU zones are used for the major contests — CQ WW and CQ WPX score by CQ zone; ARRL DX and IARU use ITU. Zone numbers shown here are the home-entity defaults; large countries (US, Russia, China, Brazil, Australia) span multiple zones and the call-area digit refines the actual zone. For authoritative lookup use QRZ.com, the ARRL Country File, or Club Log. This page is a fast first-pass reference for live logging and band-spotting — not a substitute for a real callsign database. US license class hint is inferred from the callsign format alone (1×2 Extra, 2×3 Tech/General, etc.) and the AA–AG / AI / AK Extra-only block. It is a rough guess: the FCC reassigns vanity calls across classes, so format is suggestive, not authoritative.
