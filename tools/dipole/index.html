Quick formulas the constants worth memorizing
Formula Constant Value at f Notes
Half-wave dipole (ft) 468 / f — Classic K-factor formula. Assumes VF ≈ 0.95 and accounts for end effect.
Quarter-wave (ft) 234 / f — Half of 468. Use for verticals and radials.
Free-space half-wave (m) 150 / f — No end-effect correction. Use as an upper bound.
Free-space quarter-wave (m) 75 / f — Half of 150. Same caveat.
Free-space wavelength (m) 300 / f — Strictly c/f with c = 299.792458 m/µs, rounded for headwork.
Field notes
Velocity factor here is the K-factor — the resonance correction for a wire-in-air antenna, not the propagation VF of coax. 0.95 is the workshop default for bare copper wire suspended in clear air; insulated wire drops it to ~0.93 because the dielectric jacket slows the wave along the conductor. End effect shortens a real dipole below the free-space half-wavelength by ~5% at HF; that's where 468/f comes from (vs 492/f free-space). Wire above conductive ground (rooftop, vehicle, salt water) shortens the resonant length further; expect to trim more than the calculator predicts. Quarter-wave verticals are unbalanced — they need a counterpoise or buried radial system to perform. Without radials the antenna will load but radiate poorly and the apparent resonance will shift. Loops are tolerant — the 1.024 factor is conservative; full-wave loops typically tune over a wider range than dipoles. Always prune to resonance. Cut ~3% long, install at final height, sweep with an analyzer, and trim both ends symmetrically until the dip lands in your operating segment.
