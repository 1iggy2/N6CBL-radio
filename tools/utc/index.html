Why these formats
UTC is the universal coordinator for every QSO log entry — logging in local time creates ambiguity around contests, DX windows, and transcontinental contacts. Julian Date is the standard astronomical day count (no leap years, no months, no calendars); useful for FT8 sequence reconstruction and astronomical observation logs. GMST aligns the sky with Greenwich; convert any GMST to local sidereal time by adding your longitude (in hours). Day-of-year is the contest scorer's friend; Unix time is the programmer's.
