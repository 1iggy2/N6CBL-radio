Notes
dBm is dB referenced to 1 mW; dBW is dB referenced to 1 W; the two differ by exactly 30 dB. V and μV values assume a 50 Ω system impedance, which is the amateur radio standard at every IF, receiver input, transmitter output, and instrument port — the conversion P = V² / Z only holds at that reference. S-units originated as a perceptual scale and the IARU recommendation is 6 dB per S-unit, but real receiver S-meters vary widely and are rarely calibrated; treat all readings as relative. The HF baseline places S9 at -73 dBm (50 μV into 50 Ω) and the VHF/UHF baseline places S9 at -93 dBm (5 μV into 50 Ω) — both are long-standing convention rather than physical constants. Free-space path loss is theoretical line-of-sight only; it ignores ground losses, atmospheric absorption, ionospheric refraction, polarization mismatch, multipath, and obstruction — useful as a floor, not a prediction.
