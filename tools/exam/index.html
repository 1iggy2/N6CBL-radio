    About the question pool
    Source. Questions come from the NCVEC (National Conference of Volunteer Examiner Coordinators) public-domain question pools. NCVEC writes and maintains the official pools that every FCC amateur radio examination is drawn from; the full pools are released as plain text and may be downloaded from ncvec.org. Sample only. This page ships a representative subset of each pool inline so the tool stays single-file, no-build, and instantly loadable. For comprehensive study you should work through the complete pool (Tech ~ 411 questions, General ~ 432, Extra ~ 622) using the canonical NCVEC release. Pool cycles. Pools rotate on a four-year cycle: Technician 2022-2026, General 2023-2027, Extra 2024-2028. Watch the NCVEC release calendar before exam day; an outdated pool will not match the version your VE team is using. The actual exam. The FCC exam draws 35 random questions for Technician and General and 50 for Extra, one from each sub-element group. Passing score is 74%: 26 of 35 for Tech and General, 37 of 50 for Extra. Choice order. In the NCVEC source files the correct answer is always listed first (choice A). This tool shuffles the four choices on every question so position memorisation cannot substitute for knowing the material.
