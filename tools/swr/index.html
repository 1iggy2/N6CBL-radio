What these numbers mean
SWR (voltage standing wave ratio) is the ratio of peak to trough of the standing wave the mismatch sets up on the feedline; 1.0 : 1 means no reflection, ∞ means a short or open circuit at the load. Return loss is the same information expressed in dB — it is the ratio of forward to reflected power at the load, so larger numbers are better. |Γ| is the magnitude of the complex reflection coefficient at the load; SWR, return loss, and |Γ| are three views of one underlying quantity. Mismatch loss is the dB of forward power that physically cannot couple into the load because some of it returns to the source — this is not the same as feedline loss, and it does not include the additional round-trip attenuation a real, lossy coax adds to the reflected wave. With a real tuner at the rig end, most of that reflected power is re-reflected back toward the antenna, so the radiated total is generally higher than mismatch loss alone implies; the figure here is the strict load-side coupling deficit at 50 Ω.
