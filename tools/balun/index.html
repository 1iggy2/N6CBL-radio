Notes
AL tolerance. Published AL values are nominal; real cores vary ±20–30% batch to batch, and AL drops with flux density and temperature. Treat the calculator output as a starting point and confirm with a VNA or LCR meter before potting the build. Mix selection. Type 43 is the workhorse ferrite for HF baluns and ununs 1–50 MHz. Type 31 has higher loss and is preferred for broadband common-mode chokes 1–30 MHz. Type 77 favors the low bands (1–10 MHz). Type 61 extends to VHF with lower μ and lower loss. Iron powder. T- cores (mix 2, 6, 10, etc.) are tuned-circuit and high-Q transmitting cores; they are not appropriate for broadband baluns or common-mode chokes — their μ is too low and their saturation behavior is wrong for that job. Thermal limits. For high-power baluns, inductance is only half the design: core area, mix loss tangent, and ventilation determine the real power rating. A balun that meets the choking-impedance target can still cook on a long contest run if undersized.
