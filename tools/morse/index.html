Method notes
Koch method. Introduce one new character at a time at the full target character speed; hold at each level until copy accuracy reaches roughly 90% before adding the next character. The character order above is the standard Koch sequence used by most modern CW courses. Farnsworth spacing. Each character is sent at the full character WPM; the gaps between characters and between words are stretched so the overall effective WPM is slower. This trains the ear on real character rhythm from day one, without overwhelming the operator with high overall throughput. Tone. 600 Hz is the conventional amateur CW sidetone; sit somewhere between 500 and 800 Hz depending on what your ear prefers. Audio. All tones are synthesized live by the browser via the AudioContext Web Audio API — no external sound files are downloaded. A short 5 ms envelope on each element keeps key-clicks out of the signal.
