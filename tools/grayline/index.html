Why grayline matters
Grayline propagation happens along the terminator — the moving line between day and night — while the sun is at or just below the local horizon at one or both stations. The lower D-layer absorption on the dark side combined with residual F-layer ionization on the lit side produces an unusual low-loss path for short windows around sunrise and sunset. The table above pinpoints exactly when your QTH and a target station share that twilight overlap, so you can schedule transmit time around it rather than guessing. Bands that benefit: 80m, 40m, and 30m most reliably; 60m and 17m occasionally. The window typically lasts 20–40 minutes per terminator pass, so a sharp clock matters.
