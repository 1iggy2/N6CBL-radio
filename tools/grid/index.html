Notes
Maidenhead divides the globe into a nested locator system: field (two letters A–R, 20° longitude × 10° latitude), square (two digits 0–9, 2° longitude × 1° latitude), subsquare (two letters a–x, 5′ longitude × 2.5′ latitude), and an optional extended subsquare (two digits, 30″ longitude × 15″ latitude). A 4-character grid covers roughly 1° × 2°; a 6-character grid narrows that to 2.5′ × 5′ — the standard precision for FT8, contests, and POTA logs. Distance uses the haversine formula on a spherical earth (R = 6371 km); great-circle distance, not rhumb line. Bearings are degrees true, measured clockwise from north at the From station — the reciprocal is the initial bearing from the To station back, which is generally not the From bearing ± 180° on long paths.
