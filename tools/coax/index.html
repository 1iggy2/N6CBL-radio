Notes & caveats
Approximation. Loss is modeled as dB/100 ft = k1 · √fMHz + k2 · fMHz, where k1 captures conductor (skin-effect) loss and k2 captures dielectric loss. Coefficients are derived from manufacturer datasheets and published reference tables; they are typical values, not guarantees. Real-world attenuation depends on temperature, water ingress, connector quality, cable age, and bend radius. SWR is not modeled. A mismatch at the antenna raises the matched-line loss above what this calculator reports — the higher the SWR, the larger the additional loss. Add a separate SWR-loss term if your VSWR is well above 1.5:1. Rule of thumb. At HF, almost any 50-ft RG-58 run is acceptable. At VHF/UHF, every connector and every meter of cheap coax matters — switch to LMR-400 or Heliax once you climb past 144 MHz, especially for satellite, repeater, or weak-signal work. Units. Frequency in MHz; length accepts feet or meters and is converted internally to feet for the dB/100 ft formula. 1 m = 3.28084 ft.
