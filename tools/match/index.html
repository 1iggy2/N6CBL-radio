/tools/match/ — L-network impedance matcher two-element lumped match / lossless
Inputs live recalculation on every change
Both impedances are treated as purely real (resistive). For a complex load ZL = RL ± jXL, cancel the reactance first — either subtract it externally, or absorb it into the series element of the matching network by adjusting its reactance to compensate. The L-network is lossless in theory; real-world inductor and capacitor Q sets bandwidth and insertion loss.

Practical implementation
Round to standard values. The capacitor and inductor values printed above are ideal. Pick the nearest available part (E12 capacitor series, standard inductor windings or a measured air-core coil), re-compute the resulting reactance at the operating frequency, and verify SWR on a vector network analyzer or antenna analyzer. Complex loads. This calculator assumes purely real Rs and Rl. For a load with reactance jXL, either tune jXL out first (series capacitor for an inductive load, series inductor for a capacitive load) or absorb the load reactance into the matching network's series element by adjusting its reactance — subtract jXL from the computed Xs. Component selection. At HF, air-wound or T-series powdered-iron toroid inductors are typical; silver-mica, NP0/C0G ceramic, or vacuum variable capacitors handle the RF voltage. Component Q drives bandwidth and insertion loss — high-Q parts give narrow bandwidth and low loss, low-Q parts give wider bandwidth at the cost of dissipation. Bandwidth. The 3 dB bandwidth shown above is the loaded-Q approximation BW ≈ f / Q; expect a high-ratio match (Q > 5) to be noticeably narrow, often less than a single amateur band wide.
